Interpreting Algorithm Results

Follow these steps to interpret the results of the algorithm:

1

Locate the value of the maximum Inconsistency coefficient:

Identify the linear graph marked with blue and find the point where the Inconsistency coefficient is maximized.

2

At that value, locate the number of clusters that corresponds:

Check the corresponding position on the graph to determine the number of clusters associated with the maximum Inconsistency coefficient.

3

Find from the bar graphs which linkage has that number of clusters:

Refer to the bar graphs and identify the linkage method that corresponds to the previously determined number of clusters.

4

The combination of number of clusters and linkage are the recommended parameters:

Based on your findings, the recommended parameters for the algorithm are the identified number of clusters and the corresponding linkage method.

Notes:

  • Explanation of Inconsistency Coefficient: The inconsistency coefficient measures the difference between a potential clustering and the average clustering over different scales. A higher coefficient indicates a better-defined clustering.
  • Explanation of Linkage: Linkage refers to the method used to calculate the distance between clusters. Common linkage methods include single, complete, and average linkage.
